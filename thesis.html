My Bachelor Thesis

The integration of external and internal match load with contextual factors to assess training status in football.

For my bachelor thesis, I analyzed GPS and heart rate data that was collected (in part by myself) during competitive football match-play of a professional U18 team. I complemented this data with contextual data such as match outcomes, weather and distance travelled to away games. I then used multilevel modelling to assess to what extent external load (GPS data, such as total distance covered or high speed running distance) and contextual variables could explain variations in internal load (i.e. heart rate). I further tested whether the difference between predicted and actual heart rate was affected by congested scheduling and thus sensitive to the fatiguing effects associated with it.
